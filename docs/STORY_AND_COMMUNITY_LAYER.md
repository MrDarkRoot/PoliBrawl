# PoliBrawl Story & Community Layer

## 1. Why This Layer Exists
The core product (Layer A) is serious, factual, and strictly evidence-backed. However, to build an addictive product, retention, and trust, we need Layer C (Story/Brand).
**Purpose:** Retention, community building, memory anchors, emotional connection, founder brand, and contributor recognition.

## 2. Where Stories Belong
Stories and images are allowed, but they live strictly **outside** the serious editorial core.

**Allowed placements:**
- At the bottom of a platform page (in a dedicated "Learn From Real Stories" block).
- In a related story block.
- On separate routes: `/stories`, `/wolf-notes`, `/community`.
- In "Behind the Red Flag" articles, Weekly Radars, or Dev Logs.

**Not allowed:**
- Inside official evidence sections.
- Mixed into the core red flag claims.
- Replacing official evidence.
- Making unsupported claims.
- Turning a serious platform guide into a meme page.

## 3. Story Types

### Wolf Notes
- Founder/operator reflections, practical lessons, and funny/tragic solo founder moments.
- Must be clearly visually separated from official analysis.

### Contributor Stories
- User/contributor experiences.
- Must be moderated and anonymized if necessary.
- Does **not** automatically count as official evidence.

### Behind the Red Flag
- Explains how the red flag was discovered (source acquisition problems, scanner noise, editorial review decisions).

### Community Lessons
- Distilled, structured, and moderated tips sourced from user submissions.

### AI Illustrated Stories
- Optional images for mascot/story illustration or emotional storytelling.
- Must be clearly illustrative (not factual evidence). Alt text is required.
- **Never** use fake screenshots, fake platform UI, or fake support emails.

### Dev Logs
- Building in public updates covering product progress, editorial operations, and transparent methodology.

## 4. Platform Page Story Block
If a story is attached to a platform page, it appears at the absolute bottom under: **Learn From Real Stories**

**Card format:**
- Title
- Type (e.g., Wolf Note, Contributor Story, Behind the Red Flag)
- Short teaser
- Image (optional)
- Reading time
- Link

*Example:*
"90 Days With Frozen Funds"
**Type:** Contributor Story
"How one freelancer prepared backup payout rails after a fund hold."

## 5. Safety Rules
- Stories are **not** legal claims.
- Stories are **not** proof of platform wrongdoing.
- Community experiences must be moderated.
- Do **not** publish personal data.
- Do **not** publish screenshots containing secrets or PII.
- Do **not** imply that an individual story applies to all users universally.
- Always label AI-generated illustrations.

## 6. AI Image Usage Rules
- **Allowed:** Mascot/story illustrations, emotional storytelling, article thumbnails, behind-the-scenes comics.
- **Not allowed:** Fake evidence, fake screenshots, fake platform UI, fake support emails, defamatory imagery.

## 7. Example Story Framework
**Title:** "When One Payment Rail Becomes a Single Point of Failure"
**Sections:**
1. Setup
2. Warning sign
3. What went wrong
4. What saved the user
5. Lessons learned
6. Related red flags
7. Official guide link

## 8. Contributor Recognition
Contributors are recognized for: accepted tips, accepted corrections, reviewed experiences, and accepted evidence sources.
**Public display:**
- Hall of Fame Lite
- Monthly contributors list
- Accepted contributions count
*(Note: Full social profiles are not needed in the MVP phase).*
