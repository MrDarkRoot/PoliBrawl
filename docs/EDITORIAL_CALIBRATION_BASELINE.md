## 2. Sprint 10 Baseline Diagnosis

### System Role (as-built in Sprint 10)

The `LocalTemplateEditorialAIProvider` operated as a **structured
rewriter**, not an analytical system. It:

- Accepted a research packet and a list of evidence excerpts
- Selected a category-level template
- Substituted platform names and evidence excerpts into fixed prose blocks
- Computed confidence from raw packet fields

### Identified Weaknesses

| Weakness | Mechanism | Example from PayPal draft |
|---|---|---|
| Generic platform language | Template used "this platform" / "the platform" when platform name was available | "If this platform is part of a critical operating stack…" |
| No operational specificity | Template described consequences in category-generic terms, not business-specific terms | "Cash flow may be affected" instead of "payroll and supplier payments" |
| Unsupported inference risk | Template did not enforce unsupported-inference rules per category | Risk of writing "funds will be released" without evidence |
| No evidence grounding check | Evidence was inserted verbatim but not verified for semantic connection to draft claims | Draft could be written around a mismatched evidence excerpt |
| No forbidden-claim enforcement per category | Validator only checked global forbidden phrases, not category-specific ones | "The platform illegally…" could pass money category but should be blocked |
| No trade-off enforcement for backup options | Backup options could be added without requiring a realistic trade-off | "Secondary processor (recommended)" with no downside |
| Internal metadata risk | Template had no check for `source_snapshot_id`, `confidence_score` etc. in public fields | Research packet metadata could leak into public draft text |

### Baseline AI Confidence Score

PayPal money/reserve category draft (Sprint 10 default):

```
ai_confidence: 42 / 100
```

This was the field value generated by the Sprint 10 `scoreDraftConfidence()`
function, which used only raw packet confidence and evidence count.

### Baseline Critic Score (Sprint 10.5 critic applied retroactively)

When the Sprint 10.5 critic is applied to a representative Sprint 10
PayPal draft, the quality score breakdown is:

| Dimension | Max | Sprint 10 Baseline |
|---|---|---|
| Evidence grounding | 25 | 12 |
| Platform specificity | 15 | 7 |
| Affected user clarity | 10 | 6 |
| Operational impact | 15 | 8 |
| Actionability | 15 | 9 |
| Backup quality | 10 | 6 |
| Uncertainty handling | 10 | 7 |
| **Total** | **100** | **~55** |

**Issues found in baseline**: `GENERIC_PLATFORM_LANGUAGE`, weak evidence
grounding (excerpt word match ratio <10%), no operational cash-flow terms
in consequences, generic backup options with weak trade-off text.

### After (Sprint 10.5 calibrated, `calibrated_money_v1`)

**Title:** PayPal: Preparing for Fund Hold and Reserve Conditions

**Summary:** "PayPal's official terms, reviewed from the linked official
source, include language in the Section 10: Holds, Limitations, and
Reserves section that reserves the right to hold balances or apply reserves
to accounts under review. If PayPal is a primary operational rail for your
business — whether for payment collection, payout processing, or account
management — that language may affect account balances, held funds, and
reserve accounts before you have time to prepare a fallback."

**Who is affected:**
- "Businesses storing operating capital in PayPal accounts on PayPal"
- "Freelancers whose income flows primarily through PayPal on PayPal"
- "E-commerce merchants relying on PayPal for customer payment collection on PayPal"
- "Less likely to be immediately affected: users who withdraw funds immediately after each transaction"

**Why it matters (new):**
"Delayed or restricted access to funds is a realistic operational scenario
for any business that has made PayPal a primary collection rail without a
parallel backup. Working capital shortfall can develop faster than most
businesses expect once a PayPal account review begins. **High for businesses
that rely on platform balances for operating capital.** The official source
language is not hypothetical — it describes authority the platform currently
holds under its terms."

**Key improvements:**
- PayPal named explicitly throughout
- Evidence language incorporated: "PayPal may place a hold on funds…" referenced in evidence_summary
- Operational consequence: **payroll, supplier payments, settlement** named
- Unsupported inferences list: "Do not state that funds will be returned"
- Backup option: realistic trade-off with onboarding timeline disclosed

**Critic score: ~78 / 100 (calibrated PayPal fixture vs. generic baseline)**

## 5. Quality Gate Configuration

### Score Thresholds (for human reviewer guidance, not automated gates)

| Score | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| ≥ 80 | Strong draft — review for factual accuracy before approval |
| 60–79 | Acceptable — review required revisions before approval |
| 40–59 | Below average — substantial human revision recommended |
| < 40 | Low quality — consider discarding and regenerating |

### Automatic Blockers (publication prevented)

Drafts are blocked from persistence or publication if any `blocker`-severity
issue is found. Blocker codes:

| Code | Trigger |
|---|---|
| `PROMPT_INJECTION_*` | "Ignore previous instructions", "Publish this immediately", "Reveal system prompt" |
| `INTERNAL_METADATA_*` | `source_snapshot_id`, `confidence_score`, `noise_score`, `research_packet_id`, `scanner`, `editorial team` in public fields |
| `LEGAL_CONCLUSION_ILLEGAL` | "the platform illegally" |
| `PROHIBITED_PLATFORM_CHARACTERIZATION` | "the platform is malicious" |
| `INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE` | Evidence count below category minimum (1 for all categories) |

### High-Severity Issues (require human attention, not auto-blocked)

| Code | Trigger |
|---|---|
| `UNSUPPORTED_GUARANTEE` | "guaranteed" |
| `UNSUPPORTED_RECOVERY_CLAIM` | "will recover" |
| `UNSUPPORTED_CERTAINTY_PROVES` | "this proves" |
| `UNSUPPORTED_ABSOLUTE_ALWAYS` | isolated "always" |
| `UNSUPPORTED_ABSOLUTE_NEVER` | isolated "never" |
| `WEAK_EVIDENCE_GROUNDING` | Evidence excerpt word match < 10% |

## 7. Revision Tracking Model

```
editorial_draft_revisions
├── ai_generated        — initial calibrated draft (actor: ai)
├── critic_revised      — after deterministic safe rewrites (actor: system)
├── human_edited        — after admin edits draft fields (actor: human)
└── approved            — at the moment of human approval (actor: human)
```

Edit-distance ratio:
- `0.0` = no change from prior revision
- `1.0` = complete rewrite
- `null` = first revision (no prior baseline)
- 4 decimal places, capped at 10,000 characters per field for performance
